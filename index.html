About Me

I am Malala Yousafzai

I am a Pakistani activist for female education and the youngest Nobel Prize laureate. I am known for human rights advocacy, especially the education of women and children in my native Swat Valley in northwestern Pakistan, where the local Taliban had at times banned girls from attending school.

My advocacy has grown into an international movement, and I have become a prominent activist for the right to education. The 2013, 2014, 2015, and 2017 issues of Time magazine featured me as one of the most influential people globally. In 2013, I co-authored "I Am Malala", an international bestseller.
